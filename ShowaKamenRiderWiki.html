Showa Riders

The Cybernetic Bug Hero: Kamen Rider Ichigou #1

All events happened ever since Hongo Takeshi was kidnapped by the evil organization called Shocker.
They planned to turn him into an augmented cyborg under their control. However, he was saved by Dr. Hiroshi Midorikawa and broke the brainwashing process.
These events added to the overall tragic tone of his character's journey, thus naming his as Kamen Rider. As Kamen Rider, Hongo embodies the classic traits of a hero.
He selflessly puts himself in harm's way to defend innocent people from the schemes of villains. He is willing to make personal sacrifices for the greater good. And Hongo tends to be reserved and stoic in his demeanor. He doesn't openly express his emotions and often maintains a calm and composed exterior, even in the face of danger. While Hongo can appear stoic, he is genuinely compassionate and cares deeply about the well-being of others.
He often shows empathy towards the victims of Shocker's schemes and goes out of his way to help them.

The Secondary Rider: Kamen Rider Nigou (#2)

Ichimonji Hayato also was kidnapped by Shocker. They turned him into a cyborg and gave him similar abilities to Kamen Rider #1, but much stronger than his predecessor.
He was augmented to defeat Kamen Rider #1. However, Hayato eventually broke free from Shocker's control and joined forces with Takeshi Hongo to fight against Shocker as Kamen Rider #2. He is a happy and lucky freelance photographer known for his strong sense of justice and his determination to protect humanity from evil forces. He was a skilled martial artist and, as Kamen Rider #2, had enhanced abilities, including the ability to transform and use a motorcycle for combat. Also, he is a very determined and courageous character who doesn't hesitate to confront dangerous situations and villains head-on, and willingly puts himself in harm's way to protect others.


The Updated Orphan: Kamen Rider V3

Kamen Rider V3 is a Japanese tokusatsu television series that aired in 1973-1974, part of the long-running Kamen Rider franchise. The story follows Shiro Kazami, a motorcyclist who transforms into Kamen Rider V3 after the evil organization Destron targets him and his family. Seeking revenge for the death of his mentor and the abduction of his sister, Kazami undergoes a cybernetic enhancement to become the powerful V3.
Armed with advanced technology and martial arts skills, V3 battles Destron's cyborg monsters to thwart their world domination plans. His iconic transformation involves a henshin belt and the phrase "Henshin!" as he dons the insect-themed superhero suit. Throughout the series, V3 encounters various allies, including Kamen Rider Ichigo and Kamen Rider Nigo, as they join forces to combat the common enemy.
The show explores themes of justice, sacrifice, and the consequences of unchecked technological advancements. Kamen Rider V3's popularity contributed to the enduring success of the franchise, shaping the template for future Kamen Rider series. With its exciting action sequences, memorable characters, and moral undertones, Kamen Rider V3 remains a classic in the realm of Japanese superhero television.

The Destron Rebel: Riderman

Riderman, also known as Joji Yuki, is a character from the Kamen Rider series, specifically Kamen Rider V3, which aired in 1973-1974. Originally an Interpol officer, Joji Yuki's life takes a drastic turn when the evil organization Destron attacks him. Left for dead, Yuki is saved by Kamen Rider V3 and is rebuilt as a cyborg with enhanced abilities and weaponry.
Assuming the identity of Riderman, Joji Yuki joins forces with Kamen Rider V3 to combat Destron. Riderman possesses a unique transformation method involving a guitar-shaped device called the "Saxophone-Flute." He is characterized by his green and black costume with a distinctive helmet featuring a visor resembling a camera lens.
Riderman's personal vendetta against Destron adds depth to the series as he seeks revenge for the harm inflicted on him and his family. Throughout the show, Riderman proves to be a valuable ally, contributing his skills and gadgets to the fight against the nefarious organization. His complex character, combined with his iconic design and memorable theme music, has made Riderman a beloved and enduring figure in the Kamen Rider universe.


The Bojutsu User: Kamen Rider X

Kamen Rider X is a Japanese tokusatsu television series that aired from 1974 to 1975 as part of the renowned Kamen Rider franchise. The narrative revolves around Keisuke Jin, a young biochemistry student who is transformed into the titular hero, Kamen Rider X, after the terrorist organization known as Government of Darkness (G.O.D.) conducts a deadly experiment, leaving Keisuke as the sole survivor. Fueled by a desire for justice, Kamen Rider X employs his advanced combat and acrobatic skills to combat G.O.D.'s monstrous creations.
X's transformation sequence involves the use of the "Electroizer" device, which grants him the ability to harness electrical energy for enhanced strength and agility. The show explores themes of science, morality, and the consequences of unchecked technological experimentation. Kamen Rider X is often aided by the motorcycle-riding Tobei Tachibana and the enigmatic Queen Hedrian, who undergoes a transformation of her own.
As the series progresses, Kamen Rider X unravels the mysteries surrounding G.O.D.'s sinister plans, leading to intense battles and confrontations. With its engaging storyline, unique characters, and thrilling action sequences, Kamen Rider X has secured its place as a classic installment in the enduring legacy of the Kamen Rider franchise.

The Amazonian Hunter: Kamen Rider Amazon

Kamen Rider Amazon is a Japanese tokusatsu series that aired in 1974-1975, adding a unique and savage twist to the Kamen Rider franchise. The story centers around Daisuke Yamamoto, a young man who has been raised in the Amazon jungle by the mysterious Amazon tribe. After returning to Japan, he discovers his true identity and transforms into Kamen Rider Amazon to combat the nefarious organization Geddon, which seeks to conquer the world.
Kamen Rider Amazon's fighting style is wild and feral, reflecting his jungle upbringing, and he possesses a unique weapon called the "GiGi Armlet" that grants him enhanced strength and abilities. Unlike previous Kamen Riders, Amazon is not a cyborg but a feral warrior with a powerful and untamed fighting spirit.
The series is known for its intense and brutal action scenes, showcasing Amazon's savage combat skills as he battles Geddon's monstrous creations. The show explores themes of identity, humanity, and the clash between civilization and the primal instincts ingrained in Amazon. Despite its departure from the traditional Kamen Rider formula, Kamen Rider Amazon has garnered a dedicated fanbase for its distinctive approach, making it a memorable and unconventional entry in the Kamen Rider series.

The Electric Human: Kamen Rider Stronger

Kamen Rider Stronger, part of the Kamen Rider franchise, aired in 1975-1976 and introduced the electrifying hero Shigeru Jo. The story follows Jo, a former motocross racer who undergoes a transformation into Kamen Rider Stronger after being revived by the mysterious Black Satan organization. Empowered with electrical abilities, Stronger vows to thwart Black Satan's nefarious plans for world domination.
Stronger's unique transformation involves the "Electrer," a device that grants him the ability to harness electrical energy for combat. He is accompanied by his motorcycle, the "Blue Buggy," which can transform into the "New Cyclone" for enhanced mobility. Throughout the series, Stronger confronts the sinister Delza Army, an evolution of Black Satan, and battles a myriad of monstrous creatures known as the "Deadly Kaijin."
The show explores themes of justice, loyalty, and the consequences of unchecked power. Stronger forms alliances with other Kamen Riders, enhancing the collaborative nature of the series. The electrifying hero's battles are characterized by intense action sequences and unique special attacks, such as the "Electro Fire" and "Electro Kick." Kamen Rider Stronger's enduring popularity is attributed to its dynamic protagonist, engaging storyline, and the exciting evolution of the Kamen Rider legacy.

The Rider of the Skies: Kamen Rider Sky Rider

Kamen Rider Sky Rider is a tokusatsu series that aired from 1979 to 1980 as part of the long-standing Kamen Rider franchise. The story revolves around Hiroshi Tsukuba, a paraglider enthusiast who transforms into Kamen Rider Sky Rider after an accident caused by the mysterious organization Neo Shocker. As Sky Rider, Hiroshi battles Neo Shocker's cyborg minions and monstrous creations to prevent their plans for world domination.
Sky Rider's transformation is triggered by the "Sky Turbo," a device that grants him the power of flight and enhanced combat abilities. His paraglider, the "Sky Alpha," plays a crucial role in his battles, allowing him to soar through the skies and execute aerial maneuvers. The series introduces a unique and futuristic aesthetic, with Sky Rider embodying a sleek and high-tech design compared to previous Kamen Riders.
Throughout the show, Sky Rider encounters various challenges and allies, including the psychic girl Hiromi Nohara and the mysterious Professor Keitaro Shido. The narrative delves into themes of perseverance, heroism, and the consequences of scientific experimentation. Sky Rider's battles are characterized by dynamic aerial combat and visually impressive special effects, contributing to the series' appeal within the broader Kamen Rider universe.

The Hands User: Kamen Rider Super-1

Kamen Rider Super-1 is a tokusatsu series that aired in 1980-1981 as part of the Kamen Rider franchise. The narrative follows Kazuya Oki, a motorcross racer who transforms into Kamen Rider Super-1 after a tragic accident orchestrated by the Dogma Kingdom. Imbued with bionic enhancements, Super-1 is tasked with thwarting Dogma's sinister plans for world domination.
Super-1's transformation involves the "Five Hands" belt, which allows him to switch between various forms, each with unique abilities and weapons. He is accompanied by the motorcycle "Bike Sensor," which can transform into the powerful "Sky Turbo." The series introduces a diverse range of adversaries, including the evil General Megirl and the nefarious Great Leader of the Dogma Kingdom.
Throughout the show, Super-1 navigates complex relationships with allies such as the android Human-Android G and battles the Dogma Kingdom's deadly mutants known as the Jin Dogma. Themes of identity, loyalty, and the consequences of unchecked ambition are explored in the storyline. Super-1's dynamic combat style, colorful transformations, and the introduction of advanced technology contribute to the series' appeal within the Kamen Rider legacy. The show marks a distinctive era in the franchise with its unique storytelling and innovative superhero elements.

The 10th Rider: Kamen Rider ZX

Kamen Rider ZX, also known as Ryo Murasame, is a character from the Kamen Rider franchise, introduced in the 1984 television special "Birth of the 10th! Kamen Riders All Together!!" The story revolves around Ryo, a martial artist who undergoes a transformation into Kamen Rider ZX after being abducted by the evil organization Badan Empire. Infused with the power of the "ZX Belt," he gains the ability to transform into the insect-themed superhero to combat Badan's monstrous creations.
ZX's combat abilities are enhanced by the "ZX Kick" and the "ZX Laser" attack, adding a unique flair to his fighting style. The character holds significance as the first and only Showa-era Kamen Rider to receive a dedicated television movie. The special brings together multiple Kamen Riders, creating a crossover event that unites them against the common enemy, Badan Empire.
Throughout the special, ZX forms alliances with iconic Kamen Riders like Black RX, Black, and V3. The narrative explores themes of unity, sacrifice, and the enduring legacy of the Kamen Rider tradition. Kamen Rider ZX's introduction adds an intriguing chapter to the franchise, offering fans a memorable and action-packed crossover experience.

The Twilight Avenger: Kamen Rider Black

Kamen Rider Black, which aired from 1987 to 1988, is a significant installment in the Kamen Rider franchise. The story follows Kohtaro Minami, a young man abducted and transformed into Kamen Rider Black by the evil organization Gorgom. Imbued with the "Kingstone," Kohtaro becomes the Century King, the titular Black, and is tasked with battling Gorgom's monstrous creations known as the "Creation Kings."
The series delves into themes of destiny, sacrifice, and the struggle between light and darkness. Kohtaro's journey is deeply personal, as he discovers his twin brother, Nobuhiko, has become the rival Rider, Shadow Moon, serving Gorgom's dark ambitions. The emotional conflict between the brothers becomes a central focus of the narrative.
Kamen Rider Black is known for its mature and intense storytelling, featuring complex characters and moral dilemmas. The transformation sequence, accompanied by the iconic phrase "Henshin!," sees Black donning a sleek and powerful superhero suit. The series explores the consequences of Gorgom's bioengineering experiments, highlighting the ethical implications of tampering with human life.
Kamen Rider Black's popularity stems from its darker tone, compelling character development, and impactful action sequences. It remains a fan-favorite within the Kamen Rider franchise, contributing significantly to the series' enduring legacy.

The Child of the Sun: Kamen Rider Black RX

Kamen Rider Black RX, the 1988-1989 installment in the Kamen Rider franchise, continues the story of Kohtaro Minami from Kamen Rider Black. After the defeat of Gorgom, Kohtaro returns as Kamen Rider Black RX, the Masked Rider of the Sun, only to discover that the peace is short-lived. He is drawn into a cosmic conflict when an alien race, Crisis Empire, invades Earth. Utilizing the power of the "Sunriser," Black RX transforms into his new, upgraded form, battling the Crisis Empire's warriors and their monstrous Bio Rider creations.
The series introduces new allies, including the combat robot Robo Rider and the mysterious Kotaro's grandson, Joe. Black RX's transformation sequence is enhanced, and his abilities are expanded with the use of the "Ridol" power capsules. The narrative explores themes of intergalactic warfare, friendship, and the responsibility that comes with wielding such immense power.
Kamen Rider Black RX is notable for its more lighthearted and adventurous tone compared to its predecessor. The series incorporates elements of space opera, featuring diverse alien characters and epic battles across different planets. Despite its departure from the darker themes of Kamen Rider Black, RX maintains its popularity for its engaging storytelling, dynamic action sequences, and the continued evolution of the Kamen Rider legacy.

The Fallen Beast: Kamen Rider Shin

Kamen Rider Shin, released in 1992, is a unique installment in the Kamen Rider franchise, deviating from the conventional formula. The story follows Shin Kazamatsuri, a college student who transforms into Kamen Rider Shin after a brutal attack by the monstrous creatures known as the Terra Beasts. Unlike previous Riders, Shin's transformation is involuntary and triggered by extreme emotional distress.
Shin's encounters with the Terra Beasts and the mysterious organization known as the Badan Empire lead him on a quest for vengeance and the truth behind his transformation. The series introduces a darker and more horror-themed atmosphere, with the Terra Beasts reflecting twisted versions of natural animals.
Kamen Rider Shin's design deviates from the traditional Kamen Rider aesthetics, featuring a more organic and monstrous appearance. The show explores psychological themes, including the impact of trauma on the protagonist's mental state.
Despite its departure from the established Kamen Rider formula, Shin's unique approach and darker tone have garnered a cult following among fans. The series remains distinct for its experimental narrative and unconventional take on the iconic Kamen Rider concept.

The BIO Blader: Kamen Rider ZO

Kamen Rider ZO, a direct-to-video release in 1993, is a standalone film within the Kamen Rider franchise. The story follows Masaru Aso, a young man whose life takes a drastic turn when he is experimented upon by the villainous organization known as the Neo Organism (NO). Masaru transforms into Kamen Rider ZO, a powerful armored hero, as part of NO's plan to create the ultimate weapon.
ZO's struggle to control his newfound abilities and memories intertwines with his desire to protect his friend Hiroshi, who becomes a target of NO. The film explores themes of identity, morality, and the consequences of unchecked scientific experimentation.
Kamen Rider ZO's design features a sleek and biomechanical appearance, distinct from traditional Kamen Rider aesthetics. The action sequences are marked by intense battles against NO's monstrous creations, known as Doras. The film's darker tone and the psychological journey of its protagonist contribute to its uniqueness within the Kamen Rider universe.
Despite being a one-shot film, Kamen Rider ZO remains a notable entry in the franchise, appreciated for its atmospheric storytelling, distinctive design, and the exploration of deeper thematic elements.

The Colossal Sentinel: Kamen Rider J

Kamen Rider J, a 1994 direct-to-video installment in the Kamen Rider franchise, centers around the character of Kouji Segawa. After an encounter with a mysterious insect known as Fog Mother, Kouji is transformed into Kamen Rider J, a powerful warrior with insect-themed abilities. The film's narrative unfolds as J embarks on a mission to rescue a young girl named Kana from the clutches of the Fog Mother, who aims to use her life force for nefarious purposes
Kamen Rider J's distinctive powers include the ability to summon and control various insect creatures, reflecting the film's focus on entomological motifs. The character's design incorporates insect-like features, setting him apart from previous Kamen Riders.
The film explores themes of environmentalism and the consequences of human actions on nature. J's battles against the Fog Mother's forces, including the monstrous Grasshopper Mutants, are marked by intense action sequences and visually striking special effects.
Kamen Rider J is appreciated for its unique take on the Kamen Rider concept, incorporating ecological elements and introducing a protagonist with a strong connection to nature. Despite being a direct-to-video release, the film has gained recognition for its engaging storyline and creative departure from traditional Kamen Rider narratives.
