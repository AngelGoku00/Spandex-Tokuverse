Heisei Phase II Riders

The Two In One Detective: Kamen Rider W

Kamen Rider W, airing in 2009-2010, explores the vibrant and noir-inspired city of Futo, where detectives Shotaro Hidari and Philip collaborate as Kamen Rider W to investigate and combat the mysterious Dopant crimes. The series is known for its distinctive two-in-one protagonist structure, with Shotaro as the "mind" and Philip as the "body." Together, they transform into Kamen Rider W using the Double Driver.

The duo's transformation involves the use of Gaia Memories, memory-like devices granting them various abilities and forms. The unique design of Kamen Rider W's suits, each representing a half of a detective's suit, adds a visual flair to the series.

W's narrative unfolds as the detectives delve into the enigmatic Gaia Memories, facing a gallery of Dopants with different powers and motivations. The central mystery involves Philip's lost memories and the origins of the Gaia Memories, leading to a larger conspiracy.

The series introduces the concept of the Museum, an organization orchestrating the Dopant crimes, adding an overarching antagonist to the narrative. The character of Shroud, a mysterious figure with ties to Philip's past, adds layers of intrigue and mentorship.

Kamen Rider W's fusion of detective noir, superhero action, and a compelling character dynamic between Shotaro and Philip contributes to its popularity. The series is celebrated for its intricate storytelling, memorable characters, and the exploration of themes such as partnership, memory, and the consequences of technological advancements.

The Color Combo Collector: Kamen Rider OOO

Kamen Rider OOO, which aired from 2010 to 2011, follows Eiji Hino, a carefree and optimistic drifter who becomes the titular hero by using the OOO Driver and three Core Medals. The series centers on the struggle between Eiji and the Greeed, ancient and malevolent beings, over the possession of the Cell Medals, which have the power to create or destroy life. Eiji transforms into Kamen Rider OOO by combining the Taka, Tora, and Batta Medals, each representing a different animal.

OOO's design incorporates animal motifs, and his combat forms, such as TaToBa, GataKiriBa, and RaToraTah, showcase a visually dynamic and versatile Rider. The series introduces the concept of the Ankh, a Greeed who allies with Eiji but seeks his own agenda, adding layers of intrigue to the narrative.

The core theme revolves around desire, as both the Greeed and Eiji confront their innermost wishes and the consequences of their actions. The series explores the moral ambiguity of the Greeed, highlighting their tragic origins and complex relationships with humanity.

Kamen Rider OOO incorporates the concept of the Kamen Rider Birth system, introduced by secondary Rider Akira Date, which allows for intense and high-stakes battles. The ensemble cast, including Hina Izumi and Ankh's evolving character arc, contributes to the emotional depth and character dynamics.

The series addresses societal issues, including corporate greed and the impact of human desires on the world. Kamen Rider OOO is praised for its engaging storytelling, intricate characters, and the fusion of symbolic themes with dynamic action sequences, making it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider franchise.

The Pompadour Rocket: Kamen Rider Fourzee

Kamen Rider Fourze, airing from 2011 to 2012, follows Gentaro Kisaragi, a charismatic high school student who transforms into Kamen Rider Fourze to protect his schoolmates from mysterious cosmic threats. The series takes a unique approach, blending high school drama with science fiction elements as Gentaro forms the Kamen Rider Club, a group of students dedicated to exploring the mysteries surrounding the Zodiarts.

Fourze's transformation involves the Fourze Driver and Astro Switches, allowing him to access a variety of cosmic-themed forms such as Rocket, Launcher, and Elek States. The modular and customizable nature of the Astro Switches adds a dynamic and playful element to Fourze's combat style.

The narrative explores themes of friendship, acceptance, and the power of unity, as Gentaro befriends both allies and adversaries throughout the series. The character's catchphrase, "Uchu Kita!" ("Space is here!"), reflects the cosmic motif and positive energy that define Kamen Rider Fourze.

The Zodiarts, antagonistic beings created by cosmic energy, serve as the primary foes, and the series gradually reveals the origins and motivations behind these celestial threats. Gentaro's mission to befriend rather than defeat the Zodiarts sets a unique tone within the Kamen Rider franchise.

Fourze's ensemble cast, including Kamen Rider Club members and the mysterious Kamen Rider Meteor, adds depth to the character dynamics. The series incorporates humor, heartwarming moments, and thrilling action sequences, making it accessible to a broad audience.

Kamen Rider Fourze's exploration of high school life, cosmic mysteries, and the transformative power of friendship contributes to its popularity and enduring appeal within the Kamen Rider franchise.

The Final Hope: Kamen Rider Wizard

Kamen Rider Wizard, which aired from 2012 to 2013, follows Haruto Soma, a mysterious and aloof individual known as the "Wizard." Haruto possesses the ability to transform into Kamen Rider Wizard using magical Rings that harness elemental powers. The series delves into the conflict between Haruto and the Phantoms, otherworldly creatures that feed on human despair and fear.

Wizard's transformation involves the Wizard Driver and a set of elemental Rings, allowing him to assume different Styles such as Flame, Water, Hurricane, and Land. The dynamic combat sequences, marked by Haruto's mastery of magic, contribute to the visual appeal of the series.

Haruto's quest revolves around preventing the Phantoms from creating more Gates, individuals marked for despair. His partnership with the talking magical dragon, WizarDragon, adds a whimsical and supportive element to the series.

The character dynamics include Haruto's bond with Koyomi, a mysterious girl with a connection to the Phantoms, and his interactions with other Riders, such as the enigmatic Kamen Rider Beast. The series explores themes of hope, redemption, and the emotional toll of battling supernatural forces.

Wizard's unique storytelling structure involves the use of the "Final Hope" concept, with Haruto aiming to bring hope to those marked by despair. The intricate mythology of the magical Rings and the escalating threats posed by more powerful Phantoms contribute to the narrative tension.

Kamen Rider Wizard's magical and elemental motifs, along with its exploration of human emotions, make it a distinctive entry in the Kamen Rider franchise, appreciated for its visually stunning designs and thematic depth.

The Fruit Jesus: Kamen Rider Gaim

Kamen Rider Gaim, which aired from 2013 to 2014, unfolds in the city of Zawame, where dance crew battles known as the "Beat Rider" teams take center stage. The protagonist, Kouta Kazuraba, becomes Kamen Rider Gaim after discovering mysterious Sengoku Drivers that allow individuals to transform using Lockseeds. The series introduces the concept of Inves Games, where participants enter a parallel dimension to battle creature-like entities known as Inves using their Rider powers.

Gaim's transformation involves the use of the Sengoku Driver and Lockseeds, allowing him to assume various forms such as Orange Arms, Jimber Lemon, and Kiwami Arms. The dynamic and visually appealing designs of the Armored Riders contribute to the series' popularity.

The narrative explores themes of ambition, power, and the consequences of desire as the Beat Riders become entangled in a larger conflict involving powerful organizations and the discovery of Helheim Forest, a mysterious realm connected to the Lockseeds.

The character dynamics include Kouta's friendships with fellow Riders Kaito Kumon (Kamen Rider Baron) and Mitsuzane Kureshima (Kamen Rider Ryugen), as well as his romantic interests, adding emotional depth to the storyline.

Gaim's evolving story incorporates elements of political intrigue, exploring the manipulative actions of the Yggdrasill Corporation and their experimentation with the Lockseeds. The transformation of characters like Kaito into antiheroes and the moral ambiguity surrounding the conflict contribute to the series' narrative complexity.

The series introduces new Rider forms, showcasing the escalating stakes in the battle against the Inves. Kamen Rider Gaim stands out for its intricate storytelling, character development, and the fusion of traditional Kamen Rider elements with unique themes inspired by dance culture and Sengoku-era aesthetics.

The Race Car Cop: Kamen Rider Drive

Kamen Rider Drive, which aired from 2014 to 2015, follows the story of Shinnosuke Tomari, a talented but hot-headed detective of the Special Investigation Unit. When mysterious mechanical creatures called Roidmudes begin terrorizing the city, Shinnosuke transforms into Kamen Rider Drive using the Drive Driver and Shift Cars to combat them. The series introduces the innovative concept of Shift Cars, sentient vehicles that grant Shinnosuke different forms and abilities.

Drive's transformation sequences are dynamic and visually striking, showcasing a variety of forms such as Type Speed, Type Wild, and Type Tridoron. The narrative explores themes of justice, identity, and the nature of humanity as Shinnosuke grapples with his role as a protector and the secrets of his past.

The character dynamics include Shinnosuke's partnership with the enigmatic Roidmude-turned-ally, Chase, and his camaraderie with his fellow officers at the Special Investigation Unit. The series features an array of memorable villains, including the calculating Brain and the mysterious Roidmude 001, adding depth to the conflict.

Drive's evolution as a Rider is marked by his determination to protect both humans and Roidmudes, challenging the traditional boundaries between hero and villain. The introduction of the Super Evolution Shift Cars and the powerful Type Tridoron form elevates the stakes in the battle against the Roidmudes.

Kamen Rider Drive stands out for its blend of thrilling action sequences, intricate mystery-solving, and heartfelt character development, making it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider franchise.

The Dead Monk: Kamen Rider Ghost

Kamen Rider Ghost, airing from 2015 to 2016, follows Takeru Tenkuji, a young man who gains the ability to transform into Kamen Rider Ghost after his untimely death and resurrection as a ghostly being. The series revolves around Takeru's quest to collect 15 Eyecons, mystical artifacts containing the souls of historical figures, in order to obtain the wish-granting power of the Great Eye. Takeru's transformation involves the use of the Ghost Driver and Eyecons, allowing him to assume various forms representing different historical figures, such as Musashi, Edison, and Newton.

The dynamic and visually striking designs of the Ghost forms, coupled with Takeru's acrobatic fighting style, contribute to the series' action-packed sequences. The narrative explores themes of redemption, legacy, and the bonds between the living and the dead as Takeru interacts with the spirits contained within the Eyecons.

Throughout his journey, Takeru is accompanied by his loyal friends Akari and Onari, as well as the enigmatic spirit mentor, Sage. Together, they uncover the truth behind the mysterious Gamma invaders who seek to manipulate the Eyecons for their own nefarious purposes.

The series introduces unique concepts such as the Gamma World, a parallel dimension inhabited by the Gamma and ruled by the enigmatic villain, Adel. Takeru's determination to protect both the human world and the spirits trapped within the Eyecons drives the overarching conflict.

Kamen Rider Ghost incorporates elements of Japanese folklore and mythology, adding depth to its storytelling and world-building. The character dynamics, including Takeru's growth as a hero and his interactions with his companions, resonate with audiences.

The series' exploration of life, death, and the afterlife, coupled with its blend of supernatural elements and thrilling action, make it a memorable addition to the Kamen Rider franchise. Kamen Rider Ghost is celebrated for its emotional depth, captivating story arcs, and memorable characters, leaving a lasting impact on fans.

The Doctor Gamer: Kamen Rider Ex-Aid

Kamen Rider Ex-Aid, airing from 2016 to 2017, takes place in a world where video games have become a central part of everyday life. The series follows Emu Hojo, a talented young doctor and avid gamer who transforms into Kamen Rider Ex-Aid to combat the deadly Bugster virus infecting gamers.

Ex-Aid's transformation involves the use of the Gamer Driver and Gashats, video game cartridges that grant him various forms and abilities. His forms include Action Gamer, Puzzle Gamer, and Mighty Action X, each reflecting a different genre of video game.

The series explores themes of teamwork, determination, and the power of friendship as Emu joins forces with other Riders, including the stoic Kiriya Kujo and the hot-headed Taiga Hanaya. Together, they form the medical superhero team known as the CR (Clinic Rescue) Riders.

The narrative delves into the origins of the Bugster virus and its connection to a mysterious organization known as the Next Genome Institute. As the conflict escalates, Emu and his allies must confront powerful adversaries, including the enigmatic Parado, who seeks to unleash chaos in both the real and virtual worlds.

Kamen Rider Ex-Aid incorporates elements of retro gaming nostalgia and futuristic technology, creating a visually dynamic and immersive world. The series' innovative concept of combining video games with traditional Kamen Rider themes resonates with audiences of all ages.

The character development, particularly Emu's growth from a rookie doctor to a formidable Rider, forms the heart of the series. Emu's journey to save lives both in and out of the game world adds emotional depth to the action-packed storyline.

Kamen Rider Ex-Aid's blend of thrilling action, heartfelt drama, and inventive storytelling makes it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider franchise. The series' exploration of the evolving relationship between humanity and technology, as well as its celebration of the power of gaming culture, captivates viewers from start to finish.

The Mad Intellect: Kamen Rider Build

Kamen Rider Build, which aired from 2017 to 2018, is set in a post-apocalyptic world divided into three regions: Touto, Hokuto, and Seito, each governed by a powerful corporation. The series follows Sento Kiryu, a genius physicist with no memories of his past, who transforms into Kamen Rider Build to uncover the truth behind the mysterious Pandora Box and the emergence of the monstrous Smash creatures.

Build's transformation involves the use of the Build Driver and Fullbottles, which contain the essence of various elements and animals. His forms include RabbitTank, GorillaMond, and HawkGatling, each providing unique abilities and combat styles.

The narrative explores themes of identity, corruption, and the consequences of scientific experimentation as Sento delves deeper into the secrets of his origins and the true nature of the Pandora Box. Alongside his allies, including the fierce martial artist Banjo Ryuga and the enigmatic Misora Isurugi, Sento forms the resistance group known as Team Build to oppose the oppressive regimes.

The series introduces complex political intrigue and power struggles between the factions vying for control over the Pandora Box and its potential to reshape the world. As tensions escalate, Sento and his companions must confront formidable adversaries, including the ruthless Night Rogue and the mysterious Blood Stalk.

Kamen Rider Build's intricate plot twists, well-developed characters, and dynamic action sequences captivate audiences throughout its run. The series' exploration of ethical dilemmas and the blurred lines between heroism and villainy adds depth to its storytelling.

Build's character development, particularly Sento's journey of self-discovery and redemption, forms the emotional core of the series. His quest to reclaim his lost memories and protect humanity from the looming threat of war resonates with viewers.

Kamen Rider Build's combination of compelling drama, thought-provoking themes, and high-octane battles makes it a standout installment in the Kamen Rider franchise. The series' exploration of the power of science and the resilience of the human spirit against adversity ensures its enduring popularity among fans.

The Greatest King of All: Kamen Rider Zi-O

Kamen Rider Zi-O, airing from 2018 to 2019, follows high school student Sougo Tokiwa, who discovers he is the destined "King" who will rule over all Kamen Riders in the future. With the power to transform into Kamen Rider Zi-O, Sougo embarks on a journey through time to confront various Rider eras and prevent a cataclysmic future.

Zi-O's transformation involves the use of the Ziku-Driver and Ridewatches, which contain the powers and abilities of past Kamen Riders. His forms include Zi-O's base form, as well as variations representing iconic Riders such as Build, Ex-Aid, and Gaim.

The narrative explores themes of destiny, legacy, and the impact of choices as Sougo encounters legendary Riders and confronts the consequences of altering history. Alongside his loyal friends, including the time-traveling warrior Tsukuyomi and the enigmatic rebel Woz, Sougo battles powerful foes and unravels the mysteries surrounding his fate.

The series introduces the concept of Another Riders, twisted versions of iconic Riders born from temporal anomalies, posing a threat to the fabric of time. Sougo's quest to defeat these adversaries and claim his rightful destiny as the true Kamen Rider King drives the overarching conflict.

Kamen Rider Zi-O incorporates elements of nostalgia and fan service, celebrating the rich history of the Kamen Rider franchise with appearances from beloved characters and iconic moments from past series. The dynamic action sequences and inventive use of time travel add excitement and depth to the storyline.

The character development, particularly Sougo's evolution from an uncertain youth to a confident leader, resonates with audiences as he grapples with the weight of his newfound responsibility. His relationships with his companions and the moral dilemmas he faces along his journey contribute to the series' emotional depth.

Kamen Rider Zi-O's ambitious storytelling, engaging character dynamics, and thrilling time-traveling adventures make it a memorable addition to the Kamen Rider franchise. The series' exploration of destiny and the legacy of past Riders ensures its lasting impact on fans both old and new.
