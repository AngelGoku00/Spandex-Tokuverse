Heisei Phase I Riders

The Fossilized Defender: Kamen Rider Kuuga

Kamen Rider Kuuga, which premiered in 2000, revitalized the Kamen Rider franchise with its modern and innovative approach. The series follows Yusuke Godai, an optimistic and adventurous young man, who becomes the 2000-year-old warrior known as Kamen Rider Kuuga after discovering ancient artifacts called the Arcle and the Amadam. With the appearance of the Grongi, a mysterious and malevolent race, Kuuga must utilize his various forms, known as "Rising Forms," to combat their monstrous minions and protect humanity.
Kuuga marks a departure from the darker tones of previous Kamen Rider series, incorporating a more optimistic and character-driven narrative. Yusuke's journey to embrace his role as Kuuga and understand the Grongi threat forms the emotional core of the series.
The transformation sequences, featuring the iconic "Henshin!" call, showcase Kuuga's dynamic and visually impressive superhero suits. The series explores the psychological toll of being a Kamen Rider, as well as the impact of the battles on both the hero and those around him.
Kamen Rider Kuuga is lauded for its character development, intricate storytelling, and the introduction of the Grongi language, adding an extra layer of mystery to the narrative. Its success played a pivotal role in revitalizing the Kamen Rider franchise for a new era.

The Evolutionary Amnesiac: Kamen Rider AgitΩ

Kamen Rider AgitΩ, the 2001-2002 installment in the Kamen Rider franchise, presents a multi-faceted narrative centered around the emergence of Agito, a mysterious second Kamen Rider following the events of Kamen Rider Kuuga. The series follows Shoichi Tsugami, an amnesiac protagonist with a connection to the powerful Agito Seed, as he discovers his identity and becomes Kamen Rider Agito. As Agito, Shoichi battles the enigmatic Lords, monstrous creatures threatening humanity, and confronts the mystery of the "Unknown."
AgitΩ introduces a complex storyline involving multiple Riders, including the enigmatic G3 and the mysterious Gills, each with their unique motivations and connections to the AgitΩ phenomenon. The narrative unfolds as characters grapple with their roles as protectors or threats to humanity.
The series explores themes of identity, destiny, and the consequences of wielding extraordinary powers. AgitΩ's transformation sequences and dynamic Rider forms contribute to the visual appeal, and the show balances intense action with character-driven storytelling.
Kamen Rider AgitΩ is praised for its intricate plot, character development, and the integration of psychological elements. Its success further solidified the franchise's ability to evolve and adapt to new storytelling approaches while maintaining the core elements that fans appreciate.

The Metal Officer: Kamen Rider G3

Kamen Rider G3 is a character and concept introduced in the 2001 Kamen Rider series, Kamen Rider AgitΩ. G3 represents a technologically advanced and non-biological attempt at creating a Kamen Rider. In the series, G3 is a specialized combat suit developed by the police force to combat the mysterious and monstrous creatures known as the Lords.
The G3 system is initially operated by Detective Shinya Tachibana, who undergoes intense physical and mental training to utilize the high-tech suit effectively. G3 serves as a parallel and contrast to the organic Kamen Rider AgitΩ, highlighting the intersection of technology and human determination in the face of supernatural threats.
The G3 suit features an array of weapons, including the GM-01 Scorpion handgun, the GG-02 Salamander assault rifle, and the GB-03 Condor shotgun, enhancing its versatility in battles against the Lords. However, the G3 system is not without its flaws, and its use raises ethical questions about the potential consequences of relying on technology for superheroic endeavors.
Kamen Rider G3's introduction in Kamen Rider AgitΩ adds an intriguing dynamic to the series, showcasing the diverse approaches to combating supernatural threats within the Kamen Rider universe. The character and concept contribute to the exploration of the human condition, the impact of technology on heroism, and the broader themes of the AgitΩ series.

The Mirror Peacekeeper: Kamen Rider Ryuki

Kamen Rider Ryuki, which aired in 2002-2003, introduces a unique and complex narrative within the Kamen Rider franchise. The story revolves around a deadly Rider Battle, where 13 individuals, each with their own Contract Monster, compete for the ultimate prize— a single wish granted by the powerful entity known as the Advent Master. The protagonist, Shinji Kido, transforms into Kamen Rider Ryuki, entering the Rider Battle with a sense of justice and a desire to prevent unnecessary bloodshed.
As the Rider Battle progresses, alliances form, secrets are revealed, and the true nature of the participants' wishes comes to light. Ryuki's primary antagonist is the enigmatic Kamen Rider Knight, who harbors a dark agenda and a personal vendetta against the other Riders.
The series explores moral ambiguity, sacrifice, and the consequences of pursuing one's desires without considering the impact on others. Ryuki's transformation sequences, involving the use of the Card Deck and Advent Cards, add a strategic and dynamic element to the Rider battles.
Kamen Rider Ryuki is known for its intricate storytelling, numerous plot twists, and a large ensemble cast of Riders, each with their own motivations and conflicts. The show's darker tone and exploration of complex themes make it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider series.

The Dark Knight: Kamen Rider Knight

Kamen Rider Knight, a central character in the 2002-2003 series Kamen Rider Ryuki, is the mysterious and conflicted Ren Akiyama. A participant in the deadly Rider Battle, Ren transforms into Knight to fulfill his own desires and locate his missing sister. His unique Contract Monster, Darkwing, aids him in battles against other Riders and their monstrous adversaries.
Knight's character is defined by a sense of stoicism, secrecy, and a dedication to his personal mission. His quest for vengeance and the discovery of the truth behind the Rider Battle contribute to the series' overarching narrative. Knight's interactions with other Riders, especially the protagonist Kamen Rider Ryuki, reveal layers of complexity and conflicting loyalties.
Ren's internal struggle and the moral ambiguity surrounding his actions add depth to the character, elevating him beyond a typical antagonist. Knight's distinctive and sleek design, along with his powerful Advent Cards, enhance the visual appeal of his transformation sequences and battles.
Kamen Rider Knight's role as both a rival and ally within the Rider Battle adds significant tension and emotional resonance to the series. Ren's character arc and the revelations about his past contribute to the overall complexity and depth of the Kamen Rider Ryuki storyline.

The Crimson Dream Protector: Kamen Rider Faiz

Kamen Rider Faiz, the 2003-2004 installment in the Kamen Rider franchise, follows the story of Takumi Inui, who unexpectedly becomes the titular hero after encountering the Smart Brain Corporation's Orphnoch, mysterious creatures with the ability to transform into humans. As Kamen Rider Faiz, Takumi fights against the Orphnoch menace, trying to uncover the truth behind their origins and the enigmatic organization orchestrating their existence.
The series explores themes of identity, humanity, and the consequences of technological advancements, as the Orphnoch are created by Smart Brain's experimentation. Faiz utilizes the Faiz Driver and the Faiz Phone to transform, showcasing a sleek and high-tech design. The Rider's signature finisher, the "Crimson Smash," adds intensity to the action sequences.
Takumi's character undergoes significant development as he grapples with his role as Faiz and the moral complexities of battling beings that were once human. The series introduces other Riders, including Kamen Rider Kaixa and Kamen Rider Delta, each with their own motivations and connections to the overarching narrative.
Kamen Rider Faiz is distinguished by its darker and more mature tone, intricate plot twists, and the emotional struggles faced by the characters. The show's exploration of the blurred lines between humanity and technology makes it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider series.

The Bringer of Chaotic Misunderstanding: Kamen Rider Kaixa

Kamen Rider Kaixa, featured in the 2003-2004 series Kamen Rider Faiz, is the identity of Masato Kusaka, a complex and morally ambiguous character. Initially introduced as a friend to the protagonist Takumi Inui, Kusaka transforms into Kaixa after obtaining the Kaixa Gear. Unlike other Riders, Kaixa is driven more by personal motives than a sense of justice, leading to conflicts with both the Orphnoch and other Riders.
Kaixa's design is characterized by a sleek and menacing appearance, and his transformation sequence, utilizing the Kaixa Driver, adds a distinct visual flair to the series. His primary weapon, the Kaixa Pointer, enables powerful energy attacks, further highlighting his formidable combat capabilities.
Masato Kusaka's journey explores themes of betrayal, obsession, and the consequences of wielding the power of a Kamen Rider for personal gain. His complex relationships with other characters, particularly Takumi and Mari Sonoda, contribute to the series' dramatic tension.
Kamen Rider Kaixa's inclusion in Kamen Rider Faiz adds layers of intrigue and moral ambiguity, challenging traditional notions of heroism within the franchise. Masato Kusaka's actions and the conflicts surrounding his character deepen the narrative complexity, making him a memorable and divisive figure in the Kamen Rider universe.

The Ace of Spades: Kamen Rider Blade

Kamen Rider Blade, the 2004-2005 entry in the Kamen Rider franchise, centers around the Battle Fight, a deadly conflict where the Undead, ancient creatures with immense power, are sealed into cards known as "Sealed Undead." The organization BOARD selects Riders to participate in the Battle Fight, wielding the power of the Rider System to combat the Undead and maintain the balance between humanity and these supernatural beings.
The series introduces Kazuma Kenzaki, the optimistic and idealistic protagonist who transforms into Kamen Rider Blade to fulfill his role in the Battle Fight. Kenzaki's partnership with the mysterious Hajime Aikawa, who harbors a secret connection to the Undead, adds complexity to the narrative.
Kamen Rider Blade showcases a unique card-themed transformation system, where the Riders draw their powers from the Sealed Undead cards. The series explores themes of fate, sacrifice, and the consequences of wielding supernatural abilities.
Kamen Rider Blade's intricate storytelling, well-developed characters, and the intricate interplay between the Rider System and the Undead make it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider series, exploring a compelling fusion of supernatural elements and traditional superhero themes.

The Scarlet Diamond: Kamen Rider Garren

Kamen Rider Garren, Sakuya Tachibana, is a prominent character in the 2004-2005 series Kamen Rider Blade. Sakuya Tachibana is a member of BOARD, the organization overseeing the Battle Fight against the Undead, but later becomes the temporary user of the Kamen Rider Garren system. Initially, Tachibana's enthusiasm and sense of duty drive him to become a Rider, hoping to protect humanity from the Undead menace.
As Kamen Rider Garren, Tachibana utilizes the Garren Buckle and the Garren Rouzer, a card-reading weapon that grants him various attacks and abilities. Despite his initial eagerness, Tachibana grapples with the emotional toll of the Battle Fight and the sacrifices it demands.
The dynamics between Garren and the other Riders, particularly the original Garren, Shuichi Kitaoka, add complexity to the series. Tachibana's growth as a character is marked by his evolving perspective on the Battle Fight and his interactions with the Undead.
Garren's transformation sequences and combat style reflect Tachibana's determination and martial arts skills, contributing to the visual appeal of the series. Tachibana's character arc explores themes of duty, camaraderie, and the toll of being a Kamen Rider in the Battle Fight.

The Oni Drummer: Kamen Rider Hibiki

Kamen Rider Hibiki, which aired in 2005-2006, stands out as a unique entry in the Kamen Rider franchise. The series shifts its focus from the traditional insect-themed Riders to a more supernatural and musical approach. The story follows Asumu Adachi, a high school student who encounters the mysterious Hibiki, a solitary Oni (demon) who protects humanity from the Makamou, monstrous creatures that threaten the natural balance.
Hibiki's transformation sequences are distinct, involving the use of musical instruments and the powerful Ongeki weaponry. The primary Rider, Hitoshi Hidaka, embodies the stoic and disciplined Hibiki, portraying a departure from the more conventional Kamen Rider protagonists.
The narrative explores themes of teamwork, mentorship, and the importance of preserving harmony in nature. Hibiki's role as a mentor to Asumu and other young Oni-in-training adds a layer of mentorship and familial dynamics.
Kamen Rider Hibiki introduces an ensemble of Oni characters, each with their unique abilities and backgrounds, contributing to the diverse cast. The series incorporates Taiko drumming performances, enhancing the cultural and musical aspects of the narrative.
While Kamen Rider Hibiki faced some production changes during its run, leading to a shift in focus and characters, it remains a distinctive installment in the franchise, celebrated for its cultural richness, supernatural elements, and departure from traditional Kamen Rider motifs.

The Vintage Trumpeter: Kamen Rider Ibuki

Kamen Rider Ibuki is one of the Oni warriors introduced in the 2005-2006 series Kamen Rider Hibiki. The character behind Ibuki is Akira Amami, a disciplined and stoic Oni who joins the fight against the Makamou, monstrous creatures threatening the natural balance. Ibuki's transformation involves the use of the Henshin Onsa, a musical instrument resembling a trumpet, and the powerful Ongeki weaponry, enhancing his combat abilities.
As a mentor and teammate to Asumu and other Oni-in-training, Ibuki contributes to the series' themes of teamwork, discipline, and the importance of preserving harmony in nature. His character embodies the traditional values associated with the Oni, combining martial arts skills with musical and supernatural elements.
Ibuki's role in Kamen Rider Hibiki showcases the diverse skills and personalities within the Oni community, contributing to the series' ensemble cast. His interactions with other Oni, particularly the titular Hibiki, add depth to the character dynamics
Kamen Rider Ibuki's inclusion in the narrative enriches the cultural and martial arts aspects of the series, blending traditional Japanese elements with the supernatural and musical motifs. Akira Amami's portrayal of Ibuki and his contributions to the Oni team make him a memorable and integral part of Kamen Rider Hibiki.

The Speedster Who Walks Over Heaven: Kamen Rider Kabuto

Kamen Rider Kabuto, which aired in 2006-2007, presents a unique take on the Kamen Rider franchise with its focus on insect motifs and the exploration of a secret war against extraterrestrial beings called the Worms. The series follows Souji Tendou, a confident and perfectionist young man, who transforms into Kamen Rider Kabuto to combat the mysterious Worms and protect humanity. Kabuto's transformation involves the use of the Kabuto Zecter and the Clock Up system, granting him incredible speed and reflexes.
The narrative unfolds as Kabuto is joined by other Riders, including Arata Kagami (Gatack) and others, forming an ensemble cast of heroes each with their own motivations and backstories. The series delves into themes of destiny, identity, and the consequences of wielding extraordinary powers.
Kamen Rider Kabuto introduces the concept of the Zecter Riders, showcasing different insect-themed Riders and their distinct abilities. The intricate Rider battles, marked by the use of the Clock Up system and the hyper-accelerated combat sequences, add a dynamic and visually appealing element to the series.
The mysterious nature of the Worms and their ability to mimic humans raises questions about trust and the true nature of the enemy. Souji Tendou's complex character, marked by his perfectionist ideals and sense of duty, contributes to the series' dramatic tension.
Kamen Rider Kabuto's innovative approach to storytelling, dynamic action sequences, and the incorporation of insect motifs make it a notable and engaging installment in the Kamen Rider franchise.

The Stag Beetle Streak: Kamen Rider Gatack

Kamen Rider Gatack is a key character in the 2006-2007 series Kamen Rider Kabuto. The user of the Gatack Zecter is Arata Kagami, a determined and earnest young man who transforms into Kamen Rider Gatack to assist Kamen Rider Kabuto in the ongoing battle against the extraterrestrial Worms. Gatack's transformation involves the Gatack Zecter and the powerful Gatack Double Calibur, showcasing his insect-themed abilities.
Arata Kagami initially faces internal conflicts and doubts about his capabilities as a Rider, but as the series progresses, he grows into a reliable ally to Kamen Rider Kabuto. Gatack's combat style is marked by agility and precision, complementing Kabuto's more direct approach.
The narrative explores Arata's personal journey, highlighting themes of self-discovery, friendship, and the responsibilities that come with being a Kamen Rider. Gatack's inclusion expands the Rider ensemble, showcasing the camaraderie and teamwork essential to combating the Worm threat.
The Zecter Riders, including Gatack, contribute to the series' emphasis on collaboration and the strategic use of their insect-themed powers. The dynamic between Gatack and Kabuto adds depth to the character interactions, as Arata and Souji Tendou navigate their roles as heroes and allies.
Kamen Rider Gatack's portrayal, along with his evolution throughout the series, makes him a significant and integral part of Kamen Rider Kabuto, contributing to the overall appeal of the show with his unique abilities and character development.

The Temporal Trailblazer & The Imagin Buddies: Kamen Rider Den-O

Kamen Rider Den-O, which aired in 2007-2008, is a unique and imaginative installment in the Kamen Rider franchise. The series revolves around Ryotaro Nogami, a young man with a passive nature who unintentionally becomes the host for the Imagin, otherworldly creatures that travel through time and grant wishes. When Ryotaro transforms into Kamen Rider Den-O, he partners with the Imagin to protect the timeline from temporal distortions caused by malevolent entities.
Den-O's transformation involves the use of the Rider Pass and the Den-O Belt, showcasing a visually distinctive and colorful design. The series introduces a time-traveling train, the Den-Liner, as the heroes' base of operations.
Each Imagin corresponds to a different time period, bringing a diverse range of personalities and abilities to the series. The narrative explores themes of friendship, identity, and the impact of one's actions on the timeline.
Den-O features multiple forms, known as "Climaxes," reflecting the personalities and powers of the Imagin. The show's humor, dynamic action sequences, and engaging character dynamics contribute to its popularity.
The character of Hana, the mysterious time-traveling girl, adds an additional layer of intrigue to the series as her connection to Ryotaro and the Imagin unfolds. Kamen Rider Den-O is celebrated for its creativity, time-traveling elements, and the charming and humorous interactions between the protagonist and the Imagin.

The Chrono Vanguard: Kamen Rider Zeronos

Kamen Rider Zeronos is a character featured in the 2007-2008 series Kamen Rider Den-O. Yuto Sakurai, a determined and stoic man from the future, assumes the identity of Kamen Rider Zeronos. Yuto's transformation involves the Zeronos Belt and the Zeronos Cards, granting him different forms and abilities. Unlike Den-O, Zeronos is initially presented as an antagonist, driven by a personal mission to eradicate the Imagin.
Zeronos' distinct visual design and time-manipulating abilities add a dynamic element to the series' Rider battles. The character's complex relationship with the Imagin, particularly Deneb, introduces themes of duty, sacrifice, and the consequences of time-traveling actions.
Yuto's personal quest to save his fiancée, Airi Nogami, from a tragic fate adds emotional depth to the character's arc. The series explores the toll of time travel on relationships and the intricate connections between characters across different timelines.
Zeronos' integration into the Den-O narrative contributes to the overall theme of teamwork and collaboration. The character's evolution from a mysterious figure with conflicting motives to an ally of the Den-Liner crew adds layers to the storytelling.
Kamen Rider Zeronos' inclusion in Kamen Rider Den-O enriches the series with additional mythology, time-traveling elements, and character dynamics, contributing to the show's enduring popularity within the franchise.

The Passionate Violionist: Kamen Rider Kiva

Kamen Rider Kiva, which aired from 2008 to 2009, follows the story of Wataru Kurenai, a shy and gentle violinist with a mysterious lineage. Wataru discovers his heritage as the son of the legendary Kamen Rider King. As Kamen Rider Kiva, Wataru transforms using the Kivat Belt and the power of the Kivat Bat, engaging in battles against the Fangires, supernatural creatures who prey on humans.
The series intertwines two timelines, showcasing Wataru's journey in modern-day Japan and his father's exploits in 1986 during the Fangire War. Wataru's character development revolves around his struggle to balance his human and Fangire sides while navigating relationships with friends and allies.
Kiva's transformation sequences, marked by the use of the Kivat Belt and the Garulu, Basshaa, and Dogga Fuestles, result in various combat forms like Kiva Garulu, Kiva Basshaa, and Kiva Dogga. The series explores themes of identity, family legacy, and the consequences of wielding extraordinary powers.
Wataru's interactions with other characters, including his mentor Otoya Kurenai and love interest Megumi Aso, add emotional depth to the storyline. The series' blend of supernatural elements, intense action sequences, and emotional drama captivates audiences.
Kamen Rider Kiva's unique storytelling structure, character-driven narrative, and striking visual design make it a memorable and beloved installment in the Kamen Rider franchise.

The Fangire Slayer: Kamen Rider Ixa

Kamen Rider Ixa is a key character in the 2008-2009 series Kamen Rider Kiva. Keisuke Nago assumes the role of Kamen Rider Ixa, a formidable and initially anti-heroic Rider with the mission of hunting down Fangires, vampiric entities threatening humanity. Nago's transformation involves the Ixa Belt and the Ixa Knuckle, powerful weapons that grant him the ability to counter the Fangires effectively.
The character of Keisuke Nago is defined by a strong sense of duty, a strict code of justice, and a rivalry with the main protagonist, Wataru Kurenai/Kamen Rider Kiva. This rivalry adds dramatic tension to the series, showcasing conflicting approaches to dealing with the Fangire threat.
Ixa's design features a sleek and armored appearance, setting him apart from other Riders in the Kamen Rider Kiva series. The character undergoes significant development, evolving from an antagonist to a crucial ally in the fight against the true antagonists of the series.
The Ixa System introduces the concept of the "Bloody Rose," a device that seals away Fangires, adding an extra layer of complexity to Ixa's role in the narrative. The series explores Nago's personal struggles, including his interactions with the Fangires and the impact of his responsibilities on his relationships.
Kamen Rider Ixa's inclusion enriches Kamen Rider Kiva with a dynamic and conflicted anti-hero, providing a unique perspective on the Fangire conflict and contributing to the series' overall thematic depth.

The Destroyer of Worlds: Kamen Rider Decade

Kamen Rider Decade, which aired in 2009, serves as the 10th anniversary celebration of the Heisei Kamen Rider era, featuring Tsukasa Kadoya as the titular character. Tsukasa possesses the power to travel between parallel worlds, each representing a previous Kamen Rider series. His mission is to defeat the evil organizations in these worlds to prevent their conquest of the multiverse.
Decade's transformation involves the Decadriver, allowing him to access the powers and abilities of the various past Kamen Riders. Each world explored in the series is a homage to a different Rider installment, showcasing iconic characters, storylines, and Rider designs.
The narrative unfolds as Tsukasa encounters alternate versions of himself and familiar Riders, creating a complex and interconnected storyline. The series explores themes of destiny, the impact of one's actions on different realities, and the significance of the Kamen Rider legacy.
Decade is accompanied by the transforming partner, Diend, who possesses his own agenda in collecting the powers of other Riders. The dynamic between Decade and Diend adds an element of rivalry and unpredictability to the series.
Kamen Rider Decade's unique concept of crossing over into various Rider worlds was groundbreaking, though it also led to debates among fans about the execution of the anniversary celebration. The series stands out for its ambitious premise, bringing together multiple Kamen Rider universes and paying homage to the franchise's rich history.

The Thief Summoner: Kamen Rider Diend

Kamen Rider Diend, featured in the 2009 series Kamen Rider Decade, is the transforming identity of Daiki Kaito. Initially introduced as a mysterious and opportunistic treasure hunter, Daiki becomes a central character as he acquires the Diendriver, a device allowing him to transform into Kamen Rider Diend.
Diend's primary ability lies in summoning and utilizing the powers of various Kamen Riders from alternate worlds. His role in the series is marked by his self-serving motives, often collecting Rider Cards for his personal gain rather than altruistic reasons.
Diend's design incorporates elements of a traditional bandit or rogue, emphasizing his cunning and elusive nature. The character's interactions with Tsukasa Kadoya (Kamen Rider Decade) add layers to the narrative, showcasing a complex dynamic that combines rivalry, camaraderie, and occasional alliances.
The series explores Diend's backstory, shedding light on his motivations and past connections with other characters. His ambiguous moral stance and willingness to exploit the multiverse for his purposes contribute to the overall thematic complexity of Kamen Rider Decade.
Diend's transforming partner, the intelligent gun-like device named Diendriver, becomes a symbol of his power and is crucial to his ability to summon other Riders. The inclusion of Diend adds an element of unpredictability and intrigue to the narrative, as his actions often influence the course of events in the multiverse-spanning storyline.
